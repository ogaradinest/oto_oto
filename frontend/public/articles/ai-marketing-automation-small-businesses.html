Most small businesses do not need an AI transformation strategy. They need tomorrow's post written, last month's leads followed up, and a way to show up online without losing half a day to it. The real opportunity for UK SMBs with 5 to 50 employees is not replacing a marketing team. It is using free AI tools to remove the repeatable work: drafting, repurposing, scheduling, following up. ## Five workflows that cost nothing to start **Turn one customer question into a week of content.** Start with questions you already answer on calls — "how much does it cost?", "how long does installation take?", "what mistakes should I avoid?" — then ask a free AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot) to generate a 600-word blog post, three LinkedIn posts, an email newsletter and five Google Business Profile updates from a single answer. A realistic target is 12 to 20 usable assets from 4 questions per month. **Build a simple AI content calendar.** A Google Sheet with columns for date, channel, topic, draft copy and status is enough. Ask AI to fill in ideas around the terms your customers search: local SEO, lead generation, email marketing, what you do and where you do it. Include terms like "AI search optimisation" — customers increasingly find answers through ChatGPT, Perplexity and Google AI Overviews, not just traditional search. A 30-day calendar takes 2 to 3 hours to build, then 60 to 90 minutes per week to run. **Repurpose sales call notes into content.** After a call, write down the customer's problem, the objection they raised, and the explanation that helped. Feed those notes into AI and ask for a case-study outline, a follow-up email, a short FAQ answer or a landing-page section. Ten sales calls per week with notes captured from just three could generate 12 customer-led content ideas per month. Even half of those published beats generic AI posts every time. **Automate email follow-up templates.** Most leads go cold because follow-up is late, vague or forgotten. Create prompt templates for: new enquiry reply, quote follow-up, post-call summary, old lead reactivation and review request. Store them in Google Docs. Use AI to personalise the first draft — but always review before sending. A simple workflow like this can move lead follow-up from 40% to 70–80%. **Run a weekly marketing report in five minutes.** Copy your basic numbers into AI — website visits, enquiries, email opens, LinkedIn impressions, calls booked — and ask what changed, what probably caused it, and what to test next week. This is useful marketing analytics without pretending you have a data science team. ## A 90-day plan Days 1–14: set up a Google Sheet content calendar, collect 20 customer questions, create reusable prompt templates. Days 15–30: publish twice a week, one Google Business Profile update per week, one email newsletter. Track enquiries manually. Days 31–60: start capturing sales call notes. Build email follow-up templates. Review performance every Friday. Days 61–90: double down on what generated replies, clicks, calls or quote requests. Remove what feels generic. Add simple CRM tagging for lead source and follow-up status. ## The honest view AI will not fix a weak offer, poor service or unclear positioning. But it can help a small team become more consistent. For most SMBs the win is not "10x growth." It is simpler: publish regularly, follow up faster, waste less time staring at a blank screen, and learn which marketing activity actually brings enquiries.
