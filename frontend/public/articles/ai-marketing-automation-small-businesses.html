The Problem

Why most small businesses struggle with marketing consistency

In a small business, marketing often sits with the owner, office manager or "whoever knows Canva." That means it looks like this:

  • Three posts one week. Nothing for a month.
  • Email newsletters started and abandoned halfway through.
  • Blog ideas stuck in a notebook since January.
  • Google Business Profile last updated six months ago.
  • Follow-up emails written from scratch every single time.

AI can help by turning one piece of business knowledge into many usable marketing assets. Not glamorous. Useful.

The Workflows

Five workflows that cost nothing to start

01

Turn one customer question into a week of content

Start with questions you already answer on calls — "how much does it cost?", "how long does installation take?", "what mistakes should I avoid?" — then ask a free AI tool to generate a blog post, three LinkedIn posts, an email newsletter and five Google Business Profile updates from that single answer.

Tools: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot — all free tiers work.

↑ 3×–5× content output · 12–20 assets from 4 questions/month
02

Build a simple AI content calendar

A Google Sheet with columns for date, channel, topic, draft copy and status is enough. Ask AI to fill it with ideas around the terms your customers search — local SEO, lead generation, email marketing, what you do and where.

Include "AI search optimisation" — customers increasingly find answers through ChatGPT, Perplexity and Google AI Overviews, not just traditional search.

30-day calendar built in 2–3 hours · 60–90 min/week to run
03

Repurpose sales call notes into content

Your best marketing language is already inside your sales conversations. After a call, write down: the customer's problem, the objection raised, and the explanation that helped. Feed those notes into AI and ask for a case-study outline, follow-up email, FAQ answer or landing-page section.

12 customer-led ideas/month · from just 3 captured calls per week
04

Automate email follow-up templates

Most SMBs lose leads because follow-up is late, vague or forgotten. Create prompt templates for: new enquiry reply, quote follow-up, post-call summary, old lead reactivation and review request. Store them in Google Docs. Use AI to personalise the first draft — but always review before sending.

↑ Follow-up rate from ~40% to 70–80%
05

Run a weekly marketing report in five minutes

Every Friday, copy your basic numbers into AI — website visits, enquiries, email opens, LinkedIn impressions, calls booked — and ask: what changed, what probably caused it, and what to test next week. Marketing analytics without pretending you have a data science team.

5 min/week → clear, actionable decisions from your own data

The Honest View

What AI actually fixes — and what it doesn't

AI will not fix a weak offer, poor service or unclear positioning. If your product isn't right, no amount of content helps.

But for a business that already works? Inconsistency is the real enemy. One week posting, three weeks silent. Leads going cold because nobody followed up. Good ideas staying in a notebook forever.

For most SMBs the win is not "10x growth." It is simpler: publish regularly, follow up faster, waste less time staring at a blank screen, and finally know which marketing activity actually brings enquiries.

That is a useful place to start.
