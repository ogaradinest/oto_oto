"Governance" sounds like something only enterprises need. It isn't. It's the difference between knowing why your AI made a decision and finding out the hard way.

The minimum that actually protects you

  1. A one-page record of what data the system can see.
  2. A written answer to "what happens if it's wrong about a customer."
  3. A log you can actually read, not just a log that exists.
  4. A named owner — see the implementation checklist for why this matters operationally.

If you're earlier in the process, start with why most AI strategies fail before they start, then come back here before anything goes live. For the operational side, see the implementation checklist.
